Canggu
4 days · first stop
Where I stayed

Villa Solaya

Big Highlight

Brand new and beautifully executed — from the moment you arrive, the design speaks. Clean lines, thoughtful finishes, and a location that puts you right in the middle of everything Canggu has to offer. One honest note: our specific unit didn't get direct sun on the pool. Worth clarifying when you book. Also — skip Airbnb. I found it on Booking.com and the rate was noticeably better.

Book on Booking.com — better price than Airbnb @solayavillas
Breakfast & cafes

Brunch Club Pererenan

Must Visit

Multi-level open rooftop with a massive tree growing straight through the centre of the space. It shouldn't work — but it does. The energy here is right, the design is right, and the food holds up. One non-negotiable: the pancake. Order it. You'll understand immediately.

The pancake is non-negotiable @brunch_club_bali

Zai Cafe

Personal Fav

One of my favourite breakfast spots in Canggu — and I went back more than once. The fruit bowl is excellent, the eggs are done properly, and everything on the plate feels intentional. Fresh, consistent, no gimmicks. The kind of place that earns a second visit without having to try.

@zai_cafe

Nude Cafe

Cafe

Small, quiet, and genuinely cosy. Not trying to be a destination — just a good café doing its thing well. Perfect for the mornings when you want to slow down and actually enjoy your coffee.

Ele Restaurant

Breakfast + Dinner

One of the few spots in Canggu that works at both ends of the day. Clean, healthy food executed well — whether you're coming off a morning workout or winding down for the evening. Reliable in the best possible way.

@nudebali @ele.rest
Fitness

"The fitness lifestyle in Bali is unlike anything I've seen. Hyrox, reformer, boxing, pilates — everywhere. The people here genuinely take care of their bodies."

#1 in Canggu

Reload Sanctuary

Top Pick

Brand new and already the benchmark. The design is exceptional, the crowd is good, and the facility goes beyond a gym — restaurant, recovery area, the full package. There's a specific kind of place that could only exist in Bali, where wellness meets aesthetics without being pretentious about it. This is that place.

@reloadsanctuary
#2 in Canggu

Obsidian

Gym

Objectively a stunning gym. The pool area, sauna, and restaurant are all excellent — and the training floor is serious. The crowd skews influencer and competitive bodybuilder, which wasn't really my energy. But if that's your world, you'll love it here. The space itself is hard to fault.

@obsidian.bali
#3 in Canggu

OMNI

Gym

Visually one of the best gyms I've been in. The open rooftop training area and pool are genuinely impressive. The training floor itself is on the smaller side. One thing that genuinely annoyed me: they don't let you work on your laptop in the restaurant — you have to pay extra for the coworking space. A small policy, but the kind of thing that lingers.

@omni_bali
Dining, shopping & sunset

Ina Ré Bali

Fine Dining

Part of the same group as Savaya, and it shows — the level of execution here is different from most of Canggu. Mediterranean-leaning menu with a paella that's genuinely worth ordering. The atmosphere is refined without feeling stiff. Yes, it's expensive by Bali standards. But you're not paying for Bali — you're paying for the experience. They also do breakfast, have a beautiful pool, and host big DJ nights. It's a full-day destination if you let it be.

@inarebali

Open House

Seseh

Located in Seseh — slightly outside the Canggu core, but worth the trip. Build-your-own plate concept with clean, healthy options that actually taste good. Sauna, pool, and house music running throughout. It's the kind of place that manages to offer everything without feeling like it's trying to be too many things at once. Go for the food, stay for the afternoon.

@openhousebali

La Brisa

Sunset

The best place to watch the sunset in Canggu — and it's not particularly close. La Brisa has a raw, natural character that most of the polished beach clubs around it don't. We had a table that was fully redeemable against food and drinks, which makes the decision easy. The cocktails are solid, the food works, and the view does everything else.

Book a table facing the water

Karja Concept Store

Shopping

The best clothing store I found in Canggu. They have a strong linen collection — shirts, trousers, pieces that actually travel well — for both men and women. Worth noting: I did a photoshoot with them as a brand partner. But the recommendation existed before that, and it still stands.

@labrisabali @karjaconceptstorebali
Wellness & experiences

Pressure Spa

Must Experience

The massage is excellent. But what makes Pressure different is the environment — raw stone walls, low lighting, a design that feels more like an art installation than a spa. You walk in and immediately understand why it's everywhere on Instagram. The treatment and the space are both 10/10. Don't leave Canggu without going.

@pressure.bali
Hotels worth visiting

Nirjhara Bali

Stay

We stayed in the canopy suite — a duplex with a private rooftop terrace, an oversized bathtub surrounded by jungle canopy, and a waterfall just beyond. It's a completely different world from Canggu's main strip. The service is genuinely exceptional — attentive without being intrusive. Go here when you need to reset.

Ulaman Eco Resort

Day Pass

This place went viral for a reason. The entire resort is built from bamboo — architecturally it's unlike anything else in Bali. Spa, yoga, beautiful grounds, and a calm that's hard to find elsewhere. They offer day passes, so you don't need to stay to experience it. One of those places that genuinely lives up to the hype.

@nirjharabali @ulamanbali
Ubud
Day trip · rice fields & jungle
Where I stayed

Prana Ubud

Big Highlight

The design concept here is unlike anything else in Ubud — the villa feels like it was built inside a shipping container, but the execution is beautiful. Breathtaking views over the jungle, an infinity pool, and service that actually delivers. It's a 20-minute drive from the centre of Ubud. Every minute of it is worth it.

@stayatprana
Must do

Tegalalang Rice Fields

Non-Negotiable

Non-negotiable. If you go to Bali and skip Tegalalang, you've missed one of the most extraordinary landscapes on the island. The terraces are dramatic, layered, and completely alive. Go early — before 8am if you can — and you'll have it mostly to yourself. The photos you take here will be some of the best of your trip.

Go early morning to beat the crowd

Sacred Monkey Forest

Activity

A classic for a reason. The monkeys are wild, entertaining, and completely unbothered by you — which is exactly what makes it worth visiting. Keep your food, sunglasses, and anything loose well out of reach. They will take them and they will not apologise.

@monkeyforestubud
Uluwatu
Final days · cliffs & ocean
Where I stayed

Villa Larente

Bingin Beach

The interior design of this villa is genuinely impressive — modern, precise, with a curved glass wall that opens the living space to the outside in a way most villas don't attempt. Two bedrooms sit separate from the main living area, and the pool is excellent. Found it on Booking.com. Bingin Beach is a quieter location — not ideal if you want to be in the middle of the action, but the villa more than compensates.

@villalarente2
Fitness

Bambu Fitness

The Iconic One

The most iconic gym in Uluwatu — and nothing like what you find in Canggu. The CrossFit and functional training area is enormous. If you're a conventional weights person, be aware: the machine section is surprisingly small. The crowd is surf-culture, outdoor, shirtless — a completely different energy. It's not cheap by Bali standards. But the pool area alone is worth the day pass.

@bambufitnessbali
Breakfast & cafes

Artisan Cafe

Fav

One of my favourite breakfast spots in Uluwatu. The concept is simple — build your own plate from a rotating selection of clean, well-sourced ingredients. The space is beautiful, the coffee is excellent, and the whole experience has a relaxed confidence to it. The kind of place you end up staying at for two hours without noticing.

Chela Bali

Breakfast

The design here is exceptional — one of the most visually impressive café spaces I've been in. The food is genuinely good (8/10), which is more than most places this aesthetic-forward can say. The service was fine, but given how polished everything else is, I expected it to match. It didn't quite get there. Still worth going — just don't expect the full package.

@artisan.bali @chela.bali
Dining

Teja Restaurant

Best of the Trip

The best restaurant of the entire trip — full stop. The setting is spectacular, perched with views over the jungle and the ocean in the distance. The food is sophisticated and genuinely excellent. The service is attentive, precise, and fast in a way that's rare at this level. Everything landed exactly as it should. If you do one fine dining experience in Uluwatu, it's this one. No debate.

@teja_uluwatu

Kala Restaurant

Greek · 7/10

A Greek restaurant in Uluwatu — and it works better than you'd expect. The food is solid across the board, 7/10 overall. The space is well-designed, the atmosphere is warm, and the team is clearly invested. It's not a destination dining experience, but if you're craving a break from Asian flavours, this is the right call.

@kala.uluwatu
